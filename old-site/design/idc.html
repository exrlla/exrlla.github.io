Illinois Design Consulting Website

The Illinois Design Consulting Organization is under the College of Gies Business at Illinois, and as a Design Consultant for the team, I contributed to creating the mockups, information architecture, and design for the website. The website is currently under construction but will be released on January 2023.

The Problem
Currently, the IDC website offers limited information and visuals for both members & nonmembers of IDC. However, as a new organization, it is important that IDC’s website be thorough enough to inform and attract new members & clients. Therefore, IDC asked Innovation Studio to redesign their website to improve functionality & accessibility.

research - competitor analyisis!

I looked into the current Illinois Design Consulting website and analyzed its strengths and weaknesses of the website. I also looked into other design consulting websites at other campuses' to see how they are designed and what they do well!

We had three main takeaways from my team's analysis!



  • Many of the design websites we looked at lacked and rather had their own established presence. As a goal for the IDC Website, we want to increase the University of Illinois' branding. We want to include the University of Illinois' branding and colors to make the website more recognizable.
  • We want to include community efforts to showcase the community in Illinois Design Consulting, and many of the design websites we looked at do this well by providing images, community links, and blogs.
  • The three potential audiences IDC has is Students, Clients, and Staff. The competitor websites have different audiences they are reaching. For IDC, we wan't to appeal to all three, and make sure that we include information for all of these audiences such as our portfolio, staff/student biographies, and client information.

Ideate

Throughout the ideation process, the team and I created a mapping of different pages, branding, and keeping in mind our intended audience. Our Team kept in mind 7 specific groupings in which we thought about areas to improve, and constraints we had with this particular task.

  • Pages
  • Branding Colors
  • Target Audience

  • Project Showcase

research - user interviews!

I conducted user interviews with IDC members to understand their needs and pain points. I also conducted user interviews with non-members to get an outside perspective of what the website could potentially include.

Interviews were also conducted with IDC staff members to understand what exactly could a staff member or client could benefit from the website.

Some of the main takeaways from the user interviews were:

  • There was a lack of community shown on the website.
  • The UI wasn't fully mobile-compatible, and users were turned away from it.
  • Students weren't getting all of the information they needed to know and consider applying for IDC.

visual identity!

Our team decided to prioritize University of Illinois branding while keeping a wide color hue selection. We wanted to make sure that the website was representative of the University of Illinois, and that it was also visually appealing to the user.

learnings + reflections!

  • I got to learn so much about the design process! Asking the right questions is so important! Knowing to ask WHY is crucial. A user can do anything but WHY did they do it? (Thanks Allison for this one!)

  • This was my first time conducting user interviews, and I learned so much about the user journey as you go through a product! Going from the nav bar, or understanding why a user is going to a certain section of a page is really interesting.

  • I got to work on a project team with designers from a variety of backgrounds, and it helped so much getting their feedback!! I felt as if though everytime we gave feedback, I came out with more insights on creating a new version of an interview question or story map, which helped interviews run so much more smoother!
